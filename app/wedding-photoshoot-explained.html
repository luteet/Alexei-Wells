Wedding Photoshoot Explained

Creating natural, timeless photos of your special day.

Firstly, thank you so much for booking us :-) We're so so pleased to be part of your big day and are massively looking forward to capturing some amazing images for you! You might be feeling a bit nervous about planning the wedding and being photographed (most people do!) We're here to help, so if there's anything at all we can do to help please don't hesitate to ask.

Over the years we've been involved in a lot of weddings, and along the way have picked up lots of tips that can make your day as relaxing and smooth-running as possible.

Our job is to make sure that you feel comfortable in front of the camera and that the photography part of your wedding day runs smoothly, capturing as many of your 'moments' as possible that make your day special and individual to you. With a little planning and a few top tips, together we can make sure that you are able to get the best possible images from us, as often it's the little things that make a big difference to the results we get. Here are our top tips for getting the best from your photography in each part of your day ...


Bride and Groom Preparations

You are bound to be feeling lots of different emotions on the morning of your big day – excited and nervous in equal measures!

It’s such a lovely start to your day to spend those couple of hours of calm with the people you love, having a glass of fizz and getting all dolled up.

Liam is mainly there to capture your preparations as they happen, in a story telling way, but he will also make sure he gets all the detail shots that make your wedding unique, along with some relaxed portraits of your bridal party.

Ceremony

The bit where you get to say “I do!” Liam will have been at the church or ceremony venue for 20 minutes or so getting shots of your guests arriving and the groom waiting nervously, and will then capture you arriving and walking down the aisle. He will already have found a suitable spot to stand during the ceremony (although often we are not permitted to move around), and he will do his utmost to capture both of you during the ceremony in a discreet a way as possible.

You’ll then sign the register which Liam will photograph in a documentary way, with some posed shots if this is something you’d like. Then you get to walk hand in hand down the aisle as husband and wife, ready to be greeted and congratulated by all your loved ones!

Group Photos

We know that sometimes there is the temptation to have lots and lots of posed photographs of you and all of your guests. In our experience group photos can be STRESSFUL if there are too many, so we recommend around 10 combinations of the really important people to you (which will take around 20 minutes to capture). We’ll make sure we take lots of informal, documentary images of your guests too.

Afternoon Couple Portraits

This is your chance to get some awesome evening bride and groom shots. It’s only a really short session (20 mins tops), so that you’re not away from your guests and the party for too long – but t’s a great opportunity to get some really creative and unique photos. At this time of the day in the summer we get some beautiful low light and sunsets which look super pretty and romantic. If you are having a winter wedding or it is dark by this point Liam can also whip out his flash and do some creative flash-lit images.
